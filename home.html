History of amsterdam

Amsterdam started as a small fishing village in the 12th century and grew into a major global hub during the Dutch Golden Age in the 17th century, driven by trade, finance, and the Dutch East India Company. Its iconic canals, rich art scene with figures like Rembrandt, and thriving merchant culture shaped the city. Though deeply affected by World War II, Amsterdam rebuilt post-war into a modern, progressive city. Today, it blends historical charm with vibrant contemporary culture, attracting millions for its unique mix of heritage and innovation.
